Parking FAQs

When is permit parking enforced?
 

Permits are required Monday-Friday from 7 a.m. to 4 p.m. Meter areas are enforced from 7 a.m. to 4 p.m. During campus breaks and in between semesters the “W” lots do not require a permit to park. The “A” lots are enforced year round.

Virtual Permit Holders: Be sure to park so that your license plate is visible from the travel lane.

Temporary Permit Holders: Be sure to display your hang tag on your rearview mirror.

Parking Permits

Is there any free parking on campus?
 

Parking at the Dee Events Center is free, and you can ride the Wildcat Express shuttle (part of the OGX route) to campus for free.

Wildcat Express

What are the public pay lot hours?
 

The public pay lot (PPL) is centrally located near the Student Service Center, Shepherd Union Building and Val Browning Center.

FALL & SPRING SEMESTERS: Monday - Friday, 7 a.m. - 6 p.m

Summer SEMESTER: Monday - Friday, 7 a.m. - 5 p.m.

Closed: Weekends and Campus Administrative Holidays After hours parking is free.

Parking & Sustainability

Are you interested in the environment and helping the university reach its goal of carbon neutrality by 2040? We can help you.

EV Charging

Electric vehicles with a valid WSU parking permit may charge for free. EV stations are available in lots across campus.

Green Vehicle Permit Rebate

WSU Parking Services is proud to offer a 20% rebate on annual parking permits issued to fuel efficient, low emission vehicles.

Carbon Neutral Offsets

When you purchase your parking pass, you have the option to “Add Carbon Offset” to contribute to the carbon offset fund.
